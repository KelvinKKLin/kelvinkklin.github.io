100 Days of HAM

This page tracks my progress for the 100 days of HAM challenge.

Between December 18th, 2022 and March 28th, 2023, I will spend 1 hour everyday to learn about HAM radio. I will work towards preparing for the Amateur Radio Service licensing exams, and learning how to operate a radio. I am doing this because I am interested in communication technologies, and I think radio is a cool alternative to the internet. By the end of 100 days, I hope that I will be a licensed radio operator.

At the end of each day, I will post a short Twitter-like message in one of the squares below to announce my progress. You can see the status messages by clicking on the green squares.

This challenge was originally inspired by the 100 Days of X by Alex Kallaway. However, unlike the original 100 Days of X challenge, I will only keep my progress semi-public until the end. I made this decision based on Gollwitzer et al’s research presented in the paper When Intentions Go Public.

Day 1 - December 18, 2022
Today, I spent the entire hour doing practice questions on HamStudy.org. I was able to establish a baseline and determine where I stand in relation to the Technican Exam. I was able to see 56% of the exam question pool, and I got 33% of the questions right.

Day 2 - December 19, 2022
I continued to practice from questions in the exam pool today. I have now seen 87% of all the questions, and I have 51% correct. I will need at least another 23% to pass the Technician exam.

Day 3 - December 20, 2022
Today, I finished going through the entire Technician exam question pool with an aptitude of 65%. I attempted a practice test, and I got 31/35. If it were the real exam, I would have passed.

I will study until I can consistently get 85%, and then move on to the General exam.

Day 4 - December 21, 2022
Today, I continued to do practice questions, and I increased my overall aptitude to 76%. I looked at the exam breakdown, and saw that 17% of the exam will be on subelement T1, so I drilled down and achieved an aptitude of 90% for that section. I got 33/35 on my practice test.

Day 5 - December 22, 2022
Today, I read chapters 1 and 2 of the ARRL General Class License Manual. I also continued practicing questions for the Technician exam. My overall aptitude is 81%, and I got 34/35 on my practice test.

Day 6 - December 23, 2022
Today, I read chapter 3 of the ARRL General Class License Manual. I also practiced subelement G2 questions for the General exam. My aptitude for G2 was 72%, and I was able to cover all the questions.

I did a practice Technician exam and got 33/35.

Day 7 - December 24, 2022
Today, I read Chapter 4 of the ARRL General Class License Manual. I also did a Technician-level practice exam, and I got 33/35.
Day 8 - December 25, 2022
Today, I spent an hour drilling questions for the Technician exam. My overall aptitude is 91% now, and I got 34/35 on my practice test.

I will spend more time next week to prepare for the General class exam.

Day 9 - December 26, 2022
Today, I read Chapter 5 of the ARRL General Class License Manual. I also did a Technician-level practice exam, and I got 34/35.
Day 10 - December 27, 2022
Today, I read Chapter 6 of the ARRL General Class License Manual. I also watched some YouTube videos on FT8 and using WSJT-X.

I did another Technician-level practice exam, and I got 35/35.
